True Non-Profit

A public body of knowledge about non-profits, what they should be from first principles, and how to assess whether they are truly mission-governed public-benefit institutions.

The Thesis

A non-profit is not made true by tax status, branding, virtue language, or the absence of shareholders. A non-profit is true when control, money, labor, decisions, and outcomes are ordered around a public-benefit mission rather than private extraction or institutional self-preservation.

Definition: trueness is the measurable distance between formal non-profit status and actual mission-governed public-benefit behavior.

What This Book Will Build

Body of Knowledge

Clear explanations of non-profits from first principles: purpose, governance, money, accountability, and public benefit.

Trueness Scale

A 100-point scale that distinguishes mission-first organizations from institution-first or extraction-tolerant ones.

Assessment Method

A repeatable evidence standard for reviewing organizations without relying on vibes, slogans, or single anecdotes.

Public Register

A growing register of assessed organizations. Scores start only when evidence files exist and review status is visible.

First Principles

A society creates non-profits when a problem matters but should not be governed purely by owners, investors, or market pricing. The non-profit form is a promise: surplus is not the point, public benefit is.

That promise can be honored or violated. The True Non-Profit project exists to make the difference visible.
